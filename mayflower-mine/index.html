The Mayflower Mine

Mine Overview

The Mayflower Mine was a high-grade gold and silver producer discovered in the 1890â€™s by W. A. Clark, one of the Butte Copper Kings. The property was acquired by the Anaconda Company which operated the Mayflower Mine from 1935 to 1942. Subsequently, it was mined under a lease agreement with Anaconda from 1958-1961. The Mayflower Mine produced over 225,000 ounces of gold and over 875,000 ounces of silver from about 143,700 tons of ore. The average grade of ore contained 1.556 ounces per ton gold (opt Au) and 6.15 ounces per ton silver (opt Ag). The ore was of a high enough grade to be directly shipped, and therefore, no ore was milled on the project site. Mine production was from two shafts, and two principal ore shoots (eastern and western), with as many as fourteen working levels interconnected by stopes and raises that are developed to a depth of 1582 feet. These workings were driven to mine native gold, and gold- and silver-bearing telluride ores from a steeply dipping, silicified replacement zone. Replacement ore is hosted in the Cambrian-age Meagher Limestone adjacent to the high-angle, northeast-trending, Mayflower Fault.

Environmental

Brimstone Gold is committed to excellence in environmental management. Through proactive development of an environmental baseline and water quality monitoring program, the Mayflower Project has successfully met or exceeded environmental standards for its water management program. A positive working relationship with the regulatory community has been developed based on frequent and open communication. No public opposition to the Mayflower project has developed.

Reserve Estimate

  • Proven and Probable Reserves
  • Proven Reserves: 3,429 tons at 3.084 opt Au and 20.48 opt Ag
  • Probable Reserves: 4,797 tons at 2.126 opt Au and 14.17 opt Ag
  • Total contained ounces gold: 20,771 Total contained ounces silver: 138,205
  • Data Sources
  • Reserves were calculated from the results of 5,946 feet of underground core drilling, in 25 holes, on the eastern ore shoot.
  • An ore density of 10 cubic feet per ton was used in calculations, as determined from measurements of core specimens.
  • Criteria for Calculations
  • Metal prices used were: Gold at $290/oz and Silver at $5/oz.
  • Drill hole intercepts were calculated as true widths and plotted as point locations at the center of the intercepts.
  • Reserves were calculated by the polygonal method.
  • Polygons were extended half the distance to the next and nearest adjacent drill hole.
  • The reserve boundary was extended 20 feet beyond perimeter holes.
  • Assumptions
  • Probable Reserve boundaries were extended based on geologic data.
  • Probable Reserve thicknesses were estimated by averaging the true thicknesses of the nearest drill hole intercepts.
  • The grade of high-grade Probable Reserves was estimated at 3.0 OPT, approximately the average grade of the Proven Reserves.
  • The grade of low grade Probable Reserves was estimated at 1.5 OPT, approximately one half the average grade of the Proven Reserves.

Net Mining Revenue and Production Cost Estimate

  • Proven Reserves: 3,429 tons at $703/ton* = $2,410,587
  • Probable Reserves: 4,797 tons at $417/ton* = $1,999,098
  • Total: $4,409,685 *net value after cost
  • Total contained ounces gold: 20,771 Total contained ounces silver: 138,205
  • Criteria for Calculations and Data Sources
  • Revenue after mining, hauling, treatment, and overhead costs (but not development costs) was calculated for Proven Reserves and Probable Reserves.
  • Current (late September 1998) metals prices were used -with gold at $290/oz. and silver at $5.00/oz.
  • The current ASARCO East Helena smelter schedule was used.
  • Mining costs were based on the cost of recent Brimstone development work at the Mayflower Mine.
  • Production was set at 27.5 tons per shift, 8 shifts per week (220 tons/week).
  • Haulage costs were based on previous shipments from the Mayflower Mine to ASARCO's East Helena smelter.
